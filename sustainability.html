How SP helps in sustainability

Singapore Polytechnic prioritizes sustainability through various initiatives. They have achieved the Green Mark Platinum certification for their buildings, which signifies their commitment to environmentally-friendly practices. The campus is adorned with 230 species of flora, enhancing its aesthetics while promoting a healthier environment. Over 110 water dispensers encourage the use of reusable bottles, reducing plastic waste. With more than 500 smart sensors, energy usage and resource allocation are efficiently monitored. Additionally, there are four smart offices that exemplify energy-efficient design. To prepare students for the green economy, Singapore Polytechnic offers 13 core curriculum courses focused on sustainability.

SP's Initiatives

Singapore Polytechnic takes pride in its commitment to sustainability, evident in its efforts to construct Green Mark Platinum buildings. Currently, T18A, T18B, T3B, and the SP Sports Arena have achieved this prestigious certification. These buildings showcase the polytechnic's dedication to environmentally-friendly design and construction practices. By obtaining the Green Mark Platinum certification, Singapore Polytechnic demonstrates its leadership in sustainable building practices and sets a high standard for other institutions to follow.


The certification process for Green Mark Platinum involves meeting stringent criteria in areas such as energy efficiency, water conservation, indoor environmental quality, and sustainable construction materials. Singapore Polytechnic's certified buildings prioritize sustainable features and systems, including efficient energy usage, the incorporation of renewable energy sources, rainwater harvesting, and the use of eco-friendly materials. By implementing these measures, the polytechnic reduces its carbon footprint and contributes to a greener and more sustainable environment.


Recognizing the importance of sustainability, Singapore Polytechnic has set a goal to certify all 22 of its buildings with the Green Mark Platinum rating by 2025. This ambitious target demonstrates the institution's long-term commitment to sustainable practices and its desire to create a campus that exemplifies environmental stewardship. The ongoing efforts to achieve this goal involve continuous improvements and upgrades to existing buildings, as well as incorporating sustainable design principles into new construction projects.


By striving to certify all its buildings as Green Mark Platinum, Singapore Polytechnic aims to provide an inspiring environment for students, staff, and visitors. These sustainable buildings not only minimize the environmental impact but also promote healthy living and enhance the overall well-being of the campus community.
